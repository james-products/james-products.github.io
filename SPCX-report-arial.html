SpaceX successfully lands Falcon 9 rocket for the first time ever

Summary

"This was a first for us at Cape Canaveral Air Force Station, and I can’t even begin to describe the excitement the team feels right now having been a part of this historic first-stage rocket landing,"

​ On December 21st, 2015, SpaceX launched a rocket carrying 11 ORBCOMM satellites. As it headed it's way up, it did something other rockets don't do. Exactly 9 minutes and 35 seconds later, the rocket reappeared in the sky, like a giant fireball. The crowd cheers. The landing legs deploy at the last moment. The Falcon has Landed.

​ This is a historical achievement. Launching a rocket and having it burn up in the atmosphere using an airplane and throwing it way afterward. It's too expensive. SpaceX has secured themselves a) more funding throw higher profit margins b) cement dominance in the space industry. SpaceX offers cheaper and safer rockets than their competitors. For example, Boeing and Russia.

​ Why is this so important? Not reusing a rocket is like destroying the airplane every time you fly. It's prohibitively expensive. The cost of fuel is very low - 16¢/kg for liquid hydrogen and $3/kg for liquid oxygen.

SpaceX's bold plan

​ SpaceX's ultimate goal is to colonize Mars. By that, he means creating a self-sustaining city of at least 80,000 people. One of the major roadblocks, Musk says (the CEO and Founder of SpaceX) is cost. There needs to be an intersection between people who want to go and people who can afford to go. His target? The median price of a house in the US, or 200,000 dollars.

1,000 re-uses per booster, 100 per tanker and 12 per spaceship.

​ 1000 reuses for a booster. Imagine the cost cut by this innovation.

​ This is Musk's Interplanetary Transport System plan. Each spaceship will hold 100 people and "iron foundries, pizza joints, you name it." He also mentioned, "zero-G games, floating around, movies, cabins, restaurants, more." Just in the spaceship!

​ Musk's budget for all this? Just $10 billion over several years. NASA's annual budget is $28 billion. If everything goes well, SpaceX's first crewed mission to Mars will be in 2026.

​ SpaceX's Merlin engine, the ones used on the Falcon 9, are the most efficient engines by thrust-to-mass ratio.

Short term goals

​ In early 2017, SpaceX will do a demo flight of the Falcon Heavy. It is essentially three Falcon 9 first stages put together with the Falcon 9 second stage on top. All three first stages will land, and it will be livestreamed.

Reaction

​ This landing shows that it is feasible and cost-effective to reuse a rocket carrying cargo. Like all great achievements, everyone thought it was impossible. SpaceX will be able to colonize Mars. History books will recognize Elon Musk.

​ All this was done in four years. From the first Grasshopper rocket that hopped a few meters to the Falcon 9 landing. Let's see what they'll do in another four. SpaceX will colonize Mars. Mark my words.

Deeper

But then let's say that guy grabbed another person from 1616 - the 1616 man would be shocked, but he wouldn't be baffled. He'd still understand it - not much has changed, not much has been invented. The 1816 person would have to grab someone from 12000 BC to blow their mind. Civilizations? States? Monarchies? Rulers? Imperialism?

​ As time passes, the time needed to go back in time to grab someone and completely blow their mind decreases exponentially. So will our technological progress. You can only expect the unexpected.
